Pro Arguments

The main argument for amending Article 9 is that Japan should clarify its parameters for keeping peace after the deadly World War 2. The world we live in today is not the same as the world when the Constitution was drafted. Those that are for amending Article 9 claim that the Constitution should reflect the world we live in today, rather than preserving the original article. In simple words, those that are for amending Article 9 propose that the Constitution be progressive and should align with society today. [1]

In today’s world, we see plenty of potential threats. For example, relations with the East Asian nations--China, South and North Korea--are definitely a concern for Japan right now, as they face various conflicts, such as the nuclear threat from North Korea and the growing military strength of China. [2] Those for amending Article 9 claims that the current Article is insufficient to guarantee peace, and they claim that amending Article 9 to clear mention the presence of the Self-Defense Forces (SDF) is a way to promote peace for the Japanese citizens.

Another claim made by those that are for amending Article 9 is that specifically writing the existence of the SDF allows Japan to better represent themselves in the international community. The SDF has already participated in non-combat roles in conflicts in Afghanistan and Iraq. [3] The pro-amending group claims that amending Article 9 will allow the SDF and Japan to further cooperate with other nations and better represent the nation as a whole.

In addition, the pro-amending group claims that amending Article 9 will protect the SDF’s related activities. While the SDF is responsible for the defense of the nation, they are also responsible for rescuing the Japanese citizens during times of natural disasters. The SDF is also heavily involved in cleaning up debris from natural disasters. However, pro-amenders claim that because Article 9 doesn’t specifically mention the presence of the SDF, and because scholars consider the SDF to be an unconstitutional body of defense, the amendment should be justified. [4]
